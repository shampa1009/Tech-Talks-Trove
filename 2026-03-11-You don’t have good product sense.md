You don’t have good product sense.
One of the most damaging sentences a product manager can hear.

The truth? Product sense isn’t a talent we’re born with. It’s something we build.

Strong product sense usually comes from deep immersion in a product space, not from intuition or “gut feeling.”

We develop it by consistently doing the homework:
 • Spending serious time with customers.
 • Studying the competitive landscape.
 • Diving deep into product data and user behavior.
 • Understanding the industry and enabling technologies.
 • Testing and using as many related products as possible.
 • Continuously learning from experts, trends, and real problems.

Over time, our brain connects the dots.
We start to understand patterns and even predict what might happen next.

Product sense in one domain doesn’t automatically transfer to another.

Product sense isn’t magic. It’s the result of curiosity, immersion, and relentless learning.

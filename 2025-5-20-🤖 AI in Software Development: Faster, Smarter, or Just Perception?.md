🤖 AI in Software Development: Faster, Smarter, or Just Perception?

A recent study of open-source developers reveals a fascinating paradox:
👉 Early 2025: AI tools made tasks take 19% longer, yet developers felt faster.
👉 Late 2025 / early 2026: Wider adoption of AI created selection effects. Many developers wouldn’t even attempt tasks without AI. The result?Productivity measurements became unreliable, but self-reports suggest AI could now be giving a real speedup.

Key takeaways:
✅ Developers are increasingly dependent on AI for efficiency, but actual speed gains vary by task.
✅ Task selection and perception heavily bias how productivity is measured.
✅ True productivity benefits may be underestimated because the most AI-optimistic developers are often excluded from studies.

💡 AI is transforming workflows, but human judgment, task complexity, and context still matter. Technology can assist, but it doesn’t automatically replace expertise.

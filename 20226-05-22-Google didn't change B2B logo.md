Google actually is changing many Workspace icons, but not in a separate “B2B logo” way.

Reason: Google’s B2B products need trust more than trend.

For business users, sudden logo changes can create:
• Admin confusion
• Training/documentation mismatch
• Enterprise rollout issues
• Lower trust in critical tools
• Support burden for IT teams

So Google usually updates consumer-facing/product icons first, then slowly reflects changes across Workspace business surfaces.

The bigger point:
B2C branding = emotion, freshness, visibility
B2B branding = stability, reliability, trust
So Google can make B2C icons playful and modern, but for B2B/enterprise branding, they move more carefully.

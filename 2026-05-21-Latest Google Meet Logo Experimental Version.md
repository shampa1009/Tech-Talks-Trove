Most people won’t notice it.
But product designers definitely will. 👀

Google Meet’s logo evolution is actually a great example of how digital products mature over time.

From this 👇

🟢 2017–2020
The “Hangouts era”
Friendly, playful, communication-first.

🌈 2020–2022
The big Google Workspace redesign
Bold multi-color geometry to unify Gmail, Docs, Meet, Drive under one ecosystem.

🟩 2022–Present
Cleaner, flatter, more refined.
Less visual noise. Better scalability across devices.

🟨 Latest Experimental Version
Minimal. Yellow-dominant. Almost app-icon-first instead of brand-first.

What’s interesting is that this evolution reflects a much bigger shift in product design:
➡️ From skeuomorphic → flat
➡️ From standalone apps → ecosystem identity
➡️ From decorative branding → scalable UI systems

Modern logos are no longer designed just for billboards.
They need to work:
• In tiny mobile navbars
• Browser tabs
• AI interfaces
• Dark mode
• Low attention environments
• Notification systems

A tiny logo tweak may seem insignificant, but it often reflects a much bigger shift in how technology, user behavior, and digital ecosystems are changing.

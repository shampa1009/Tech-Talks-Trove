“More sales” does not always mean “more profit.”
The latest footwear industry numbers from Bangladesh prove exactly that.

In Q1 2026:
👉 Apex Footwear generated BDT 616 crore in revenue
👉 Bata generated BDT 380 crore in revenue
Yet…
📈 Bata made over BDT 37 crore profit
📉 Apex made just above BDT 1 crore profit

That means: Apex sold ~62% more, but Bata earned ~36x higher profit.
This is a powerful reminder for every founder, operator, and product leader that Revenue is vanity. Margin, efficiency, and financial structure drive sustainability.

Why did this happen? Production Cost Efficiency:
👉 Bata spent ~54% of revenue on production
👉 Apex spent ~79%

So for every BDT 100 earned:
🟢 Bata retained BDT 46 before other expenses
🔴 Apex retained only BDT 21

🌍 Apex is heavily export-driven, while Bata is primarily local-market focused.

Export businesses often face:
🔸Higher raw material costs
🔸Global pricing pressure
🔸Currency volatility
🔸Advance taxes
🔸Compliance overhead
Scale increases complexity.

💸 Debt Dependency
 👉 Apex paid ~BDT 17 crore in loan interest in just 3 months.
 👉 Bata paid only ~BDT 6.25 crore.
Cash flow discipline quietly compounds over time.

A company can dominate sales charts and still struggle with profitability if:
🔴 Weak cost structure
🔴 Expensive financing
🔴 Inefficient operational execution

As product and business teams, we often celebrate growth metrics:
 ✔ Gross Merchandise Value
 ✔ Revenue
 ✔ User acquisition
 ✔ Market expansion

But eventually, the business model asks one brutal question:
“How much value do we actually keep?”
That answer separates scalable businesses from fragile ones.

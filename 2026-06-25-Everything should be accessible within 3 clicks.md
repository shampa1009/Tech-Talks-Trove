Everything should be accessible within 3 clicks.

Sounds logical. But real user behavior says otherwise.
Users don’t actually care about the number of clicks.
They care about one thing: “Am I moving closer to my goal?”
A product with 2 confusing clicks feels worse than a product with 5 clear and intuitive steps.

Research from usability studies found:
• Users do NOT quit after 3 clicks
• More clicks do NOT reduce satisfaction
• Fewer clicks do NOT automatically mean better UX

In fact, Jakob Nielsen once found that users were 600% more successful after products were moved deeper into navigation.
Why? Because the navigation became clearer.
This is where “information scent” matters.

When labels, categories, and actions clearly signal what comes next, users feel confident continuing, even if the journey takes a few extra steps.

Good UX is not about reducing clicks at all costs. It’s about reducing confusion. Every interaction should answer:
✅ “Where am I?”
✅ “What happens next?”
✅ “Am I heading in the right direction?”

🎯 That’s why modern product design is shifting from: “minimum clicks” to “maximum clarity.”

Sometimes adding one extra step actually improves usability. Because users would rather take a longer clear road than a shorter confusing one.

Many businesses think Meta Ads fail because of budget.

In reality, most campaigns fail because they don’t understand the customer journey. Running ads without understanding the funnel is like throwing arrows in the dark.

A typical conversion funnel looks something like this:
1,000 people visit the landing page
↓
500 view the product/content
↓
100 add to cart
↓
50 initiate checkout
↓
Even fewer complete the purchase

This drop-off is normal.

The real job of a marketer is not just launching ads, it’s understanding WHY users leave at each stage.

That’s why Meta’s standard events matter so much:
• Page View
• View Content
• Add to Cart
• Initiate Checkout
• Purchase
• Lead
These are not just “tracking events.” They are behavioral signals.

The better we pixel understands customer behavior, the smarter ad delivery becomes.

Even with Android privacy updates and rising CPMs (Digital Marketing Cost Per Mille), one thing hasn’t changed:
Businesses that deeply understand funnel logic and customer intent still outperform others consistently.
Because successful marketing is less about “boosting posts” and more about understanding human behavior.

The platform may evolve. But customer psychology remains the same.

🤖 AI is transforming work, but are our jobs really at risk?

Tech giants like Block, Atlassian, Meta, and Amazon are citing AI as a reason for major layoffs. Thousands of engineers are losing their jobs, yet the reality on the ground is more nuanced:
👉 AI can assist, but it cannot replace the deep knowledge and decision-making skills of experienced teams.
👉 Studies show that even with AI tools, productivity gains are modest and sometimes tasks take longer.
👉 There’s a growing disconnect between executives’ perception of AI and what employees experience daily.

What really matters:
👉 Understanding which tasks AI can augment versus replace
👉 Retaining context, expertise, and human judgment
👉 Planning layoffs carefully rather than using AI as a convenient cover story

💡 AI is a powerful tool, but treating it as a justification for mass layoffs risks losing institutional knowledge and morale. Productivity isn’t just about speed. It’s about doing the right work, with the right people, in the right context.

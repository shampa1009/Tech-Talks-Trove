Ignoring accessibility becomes expensive later.

Accessibility is not about adding extra features. It’s about designing products thoughtfully from the beginning.
Things like:
✅ Readable typography
✅ Proper color contrast
✅ Captions
✅ Navigation
✅ Clear button labels
…benefit everyone, not just people with disabilities.

Good accessibility often leads to:
✅Better SEO
✅ Faster loading websites
✅ Better mobile usability
✅ Wider audience reach
✅ Easier maintenance
✅ Better overall UX

The best part?
Building accessible products from scratch usually costs almost the same as building inaccessible ones. Retrofitting accessibility later is what becomes expensive.

Accessibility is not a compliance checklist. It’s designing products that more humans can actually use. And honestly, that should already be the default mindset of good product teams. It’s a sign of mature product thinking.

Bangladesh’s biggest digital transformation challenge may not be technology.

It may be integration.

Today, citizens still navigate separate systems for:
• Birth Registration
• NID
• Passport
• Government verification services

Each operates like an isolated “island of information.”

The result?
Duplicate data, endless verification, manual paperwork, delays, and frustration.

A recent proposal outlined something ambitious but highly practical:
A 90-day roadmap to integrate citizen services into a connected national identity ecosystem.

The idea is surprisingly simple:

Instead of replacing existing databases,
connect them intelligently through secure APIs and a National Connectivity Bus.

Key highlights from the proposed roadmap:

• First 30 Days → Governance & Legal Framework
Create a national taskforce, define data-sharing rules, and establish a Personal Data Protection framework.

• Next 30 Days → Technical Architecture
Connect systems using middleware/API infrastructure while keeping existing databases intact.

• Final 30 Days → Pilot & Deployment
Launch a district-level pilot where citizens can access services using only NID-based verification.

One particularly important idea:
Using NID as the primary citizen identifier while securely mapping it with Birth Registration and Passport data.

This is bigger than digitization.

It’s about reducing friction between citizens and the state.

When government systems communicate properly:
• services become faster,
• verification becomes simpler,
• corruption opportunities decrease,
• and public trust improves.

The most interesting part?
The article argues the challenge is no longer technological.

It’s coordination, governance, and execution.

And honestly, that’s true for many large-scale digital transformation projects today.

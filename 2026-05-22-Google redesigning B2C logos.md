Google is quietly redesigning almost every B2C-facing product logo - Gmail, Meet, Drive, Sheets, Calendar… and honestly, it says a lot about where tech is heading.

The old era of “flat utility apps” is fading.

Now everything is becoming:
✨ Softer
✨ Gradient-based
✨ AI-connected
✨ Ecosystem-first

What’s interesting is that this isn’t just a visual refresh.
It’s strategic psychology.

When all products share a similar visual language, users subconsciously stop seeing them as separate tools and start seeing them as one connected intelligent workspace. That’s exactly what Google wants in the Gemini era.

But there’s also a tradeoff: The more unified the icons become, the harder they are to distinguish instantly.

The new redesign seems to fix that a bit:
• Stronger dominant colors
• Simpler silhouettes
• Softer gradients
• More personality per product

It’s Google repositioning itself from: “a collection of apps” → “an AI-powered operating system for work and life.”

Design trends always reveal product strategy before press releases do.
And Google’s new icons are basically saying: “The AI ecosystem era has officially started.”

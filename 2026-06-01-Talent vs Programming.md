One thing I’ve noticed while working with teams, products, projects and people:
The biggest difference between people isn’t Talent. It’s their early Programming.

Some children grow up hearing conversations about:
• Opportunities
• Investments
• Building things
• Solving problems
• Taking calculated risks

Others grow up hearing:
• Scarcity
• Fear of failure
• Financial pressure
• Be realistic
• Just find a stable job

📌 Over time, these conversations quietly shape how people think about ownership, risk, and value creation.

One mindset says: “I can build something.”
The other says: “I need permission and security first.”

As Product Managers, we see this play out everywhere.
Some people naturally look for:
• Systems
• Leverage
• Experimentation
• Long-term value creation
Others optimize only for:
• Immediate stability
• Predictable outcomes
• Short-term survival

Neither mindset comes out of nowhere. It’s usually inherited long before someone enters the workplace.
This made me realize: The real wealth gap is not only financial. It’s psychological.

🎯 And perhaps the most important thing parents and educators can teach is not “how to earn money”, but how to think about possibility.

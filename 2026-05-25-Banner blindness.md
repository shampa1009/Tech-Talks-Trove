If It Looks Like an Ad, Users Will Ignore It.

“If it’s important, make it bigger and flashier.”
Ironically, users often ignore it.
Why? Banner blindness.

Users automatically skip elements that look like ads:
🚫 Flashy graphics
🚫 Oversized banners
🚫 Distracting animations
🚫 Ad-like boxes
Even if the content is actually important.

Great products like Google, Apple, Notion, and Stripe do the opposite.
Their key actions are: simple, clean, clear and well-placed.

Good design guides attention.
Bad design screams for it.
And users ignore things that scream.

The goal is not to make users notice everything.
It’s to help them notice the right thing.

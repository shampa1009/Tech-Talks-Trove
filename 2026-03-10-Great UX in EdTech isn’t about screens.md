Great UX in EdTech isn’t about screens. It’s about the learning journey.

In a Bangladeshi EdTech product, learners are highly diverse.
Each user comes with different goals, habits, and ways of accessing the platform.

When designing the experience, we had to think beyond UI and focus on how students actually move through their learning journey.

Our users included:
 • New students discovering digital learning for the first time.
 • Returning learners are expecting faster MCQ/CQ practice and revision.
 • Mobile app users studying in short time gaps.
 • Web-based users who prefer structured practice on larger screens.
 • Busy admission candidates preparing under intense time pressure.

Designing for all of them meant going beyond individual screens.

Instead, we focused on journey design; mapping how students discover content, practice MCQs, track progress, and keep returning.

This helped us answer key product questions:
 • Why will students come back to practice every day?
 • How can we reduce drop-offs between practice, revision, and exams?
 • How do we maintain a consistent learning experience across app and web?

Because in EdTech, great UX isn’t built screen by screen.
It’s designed around a learning journey that keeps students progressing.
